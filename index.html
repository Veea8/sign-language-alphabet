What is sign language or specifically ASL?

Sign language is a visual language that is used by the deaf and hard-of-hearing communities to communicate with one another. It is a complex and nuanced language that relies on the use of hand gestures, facial expressions, and body movements to convey meaning. American Sign Language (ASL) is one of the most widely used sign languages in North America. It is a complete and distinct language that has its own grammar, syntax, and vocabulary. ASL is used by millions of people in the United States and Canada, and it continues to be an essential tool for deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals to communicate and connect with others.
